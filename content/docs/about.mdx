# About StegnoHide

StegnoHide is a small, focused web app for hiding secret messages inside ordinary-looking cover text or inside a PNG image.

## Design Principles

<div className="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
<Card icon={<IconShieldCheck className="size-5" weight="duotone" />} title="Client-side only">
  No server, no upload step, no telemetry. Your cover, your secret, and your passphrase never leave your device.
</Card>
<Card icon={<IconBook className="size-5" weight="duotone" />} title="Standards-based">
  The cryptography is Web Crypto. The compression is zlib. The text encoding uses Unicode. There is no proprietary format.
</Card>
<Card icon={<IconCode className="size-5" weight="duotone" />} title="Auditable">
  The full source is small enough to read in an afternoon. There are no minified blobs, no dynamic imports from CDNs, and no `eval`.
</Card>
<Card icon={<IconEyeOff className="size-5" weight="duotone" />} title="Quiet">
  The output looks like the cover. The cover looks like the cover. Nothing visual or structural is changed that would visibly betray the presence of a hidden message.
</Card>
</div>

## Non-Goals

<div className="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
<Card title="Not a secure messenger">
  StegnoHide does not transmit messages. It only embeds and extracts them.
</Card>
<Card title="Not a substitute for encryption">
  Steganography hides the *existence* of a message. Use the passphrase option (AES-256-GCM) to also hide the *content*.
</Card>
<Card title="Not resistant to platform-level stripping">
  Most chat apps, social networks, and rich-text editors will mangle zero-width characters and re-encode images. See [Limitations](/docs/limitations) before relying on this for a real exchange.
</Card>
<Card title="Not an anonymity tool">
  Hiding a message in a cover does not anonymize you. Use Tor, a VPN, or whatever else you need for transport-layer anonymity.
</Card>
</div>

## When To Use It

<Accordions type="multiple">
  <Accordion title="Fun notes that look like ordinary text">
    A casual reader sees only the cover. A motivated analyst running frequency analysis on the ZW characters might detect that *something* is hidden, but the content is unreadable without the passphrase.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Watermarking a draft">
    Embed an author tag in a copy you share. The recipient (or anyone with the tool) can extract it, but a casual viewer will not see anything different.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Demos and teaching">
    Demonstrate steganography in a class or talk. The full source is small enough to walk through in one session.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Small payloads over a preserving channel">
    Send a small message through a channel where stego survives and the recipient has the tool (or instructions) to extract it.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>

## When Not To Use It

<Callout type="warn">
The tool assumes the channel **preserves** the carrier. If you have not verified the round trip, do not rely on it.
</Callout>

- The channel is lossy or normalizing and you have not verified the round-trip works.
- The threat model requires strong deniability. ZW-character frequency analysis is a well-known detection method.
- You need plausible deniability against a forensic analyst with the cover source — they can diff.
